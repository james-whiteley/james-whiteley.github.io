A Future With Basecamp

I would like my role in Basecamp’s future to first involve the following objectives:

  • Work to incrementally refactor Basecamp to be able to function almost completely offline.
  • With the maturation of WebAssembly (my choice of newer web technology I like) work with other teams to push portions of the Rails applications’ computation to the browser.

I believe Basecamp’s role in my future will involve allowing me to produce great work, creating and developing tools and frameworks that simplify the lives of programmers everywhere. It would make a refreshing change to be surrounded by a team that forgoes complexity. In my opinion, your attitude to work and its place in the lives of your colleagues is the reason you have consistently produced over the years.

A major draw is Basecamp’s stability. As somebody who has felt the pain of fiscally irresponsible founders - figuratively - making it rain, I would give my right arm to work at a company that knows when to spend and when to not. Ever since I read ReWork, I’ve been a huge fan of Basecamp’s work. I even re-listen to the audiobook every couple of months to ‘reset’ when a job has taken its toll. But I’m going to stop there before I go full fanboy, and move on.

Basecamp’s Work

My favourite project to come out of the Research & Fidelity team has got to be Stimulus. The ease with which you can read HTML and know exactly what Javascript should be doing in the browser, is incredible, and such a departure from the spaghetti code typical of the likes of jQuery. I commend you both Javan and Sam!

Turbolinks and Trix may not be everybody’s cups of tea, but what they were designed to do, they do wonderfully. I respect how as a company, you don’t try to make everything for everyone.
